# E-E-A-T Framework (September 2025)

## Overview

E-E-A-T stands for **Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness**. It's Google's framework for evaluating content quality, used by Quality Raters to assess Search results.

## Experience (Added December 2022)

### Definition
Does the content demonstrate first-hand experience with the topic?

### Signals
- First-person knowledge demonstrated
- Original research or data
- Personal anecdotes where relevant
- Photos/videos of actual experience
- Case studies with real results

### Examples
- Product review from someone who actually used it
- Travel guide from someone who visited
- Tutorial from someone who implemented it

## Expertise

### Definition
Is the content created by someone with recognized expertise in the field?

### Signals
- Author credentials visible
- Professional qualifications
- Industry awards/recognition
- Publications in reputable sources
- Speaking engagements
- Certifications

### YMYL (Your Money Your Life)
Higher expertise required for:
- Medical/health content
- Financial advice
- Legal information
- News on important topics

## Authoritativeness

### Definition
Is the creator/site a recognized authority on the topic?

### Signals
- External citations and references
- Mentions by other authorities
- Wikipedia entry
- Industry awards
- Expert quotes
- Press coverage

### Building Authority
- Get cited by authoritative sources
- Build brand mentions
- Earn industry recognition
- Create link-worthy content

## Trustworthiness

### Definition
Can users trust the content and the site?

### Signals
- Clear contact information
- About page with real people
- Privacy policy
- Terms of service
- HTTPS security
- Source citations
- Correction policy
- Date stamps

### Red Flags
- Anonymous authors
- No contact information
- Hidden ownership
- Misleading claims
- Excessive ads

## Scoring (0-23 points)

| Category | Max Points |
|----------|------------|
| Experience | 5 |
| Expertise | 6 |
| Authoritativeness | 6 |
| Trustworthiness | 6 |

## Evaluation Checklist

### Experience
- [ ] First-hand experience evident
- [ ] Original research/data included
- [ ] Real examples/case studies
- [ ] Personal insights where appropriate

### Expertise
- [ ] Author byline present
- [ ] Author credentials displayed
- [ ] Subject matter depth
- [ ] Technical accuracy

### Authoritativeness
- [ ] External citations
- [ ] Industry recognition
- [ ] Expert contributions
- [ ] Press mentions

### Trustworthiness
- [ ] Contact page
- [ ] About page
- [ ] Privacy policy
- [ ] Source citations
- [ ] Publication dates
